The following cars were weighed on the following dates outbound from Altoona (Juniata Scales), based on the provided forms C. T. 1098 and 1098B, "Daily Report of Carloads Weighed and Forwarded".
All cars were carrying loads of crude fire clay from Oreminea, PA except for a single carload of coal as noted.
The class of each car, not specified in these documents, is provided based on various PRR freight rosters.
The shipper of each car is listed, followed by the consignee.

Two shipments below, on 4/28/33 and 8/23/33 are preceded by a header, "Prepaid Freight Bill". These were shipments from the Thomas Sant & Sons Co. to a customer in Ontario. These export shipments had to be prepaid; these are most likely the shipper's copies of the freight bills. Images for these bills are found in a separate folder.

NOTE:
Poor old Thomas Sant has been named with various middle initials throughout these records, including M, S and H, and the following "Sons" is not always pluralized. The correct name for this shipper is unknown as no siding is listed for any variant of Thomas Sant in either the 1923 or 1945 editions of C. T. 1000 at Oreminea, PA. Evidently he was not in business for very long. He is listed only as Thomas Sant below.
